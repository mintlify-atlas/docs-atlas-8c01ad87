---
title: "Change your Satochip PIN, label, and factory reset"
description: "How to change your Satochip PIN, edit its label, verify card authenticity, view card info, and perform a factory reset using Satochip-Utils."
---

Once your Satochip is set up, Satochip-Utils gives you ongoing management tools accessible from the sidebar: change your PIN, rename the card, verify it's genuine, inspect its status, or wipe it back to factory defaults.

## Change PIN

You can update your PIN at any time after the card has been initialized.

**PIN rules for the new PIN:**
- Minimum 4 characters
- Maximum 16 characters
- Letters, numbers, and symbols are all accepted

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Change PIN">
    With your card inserted, click **Change Pin** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter your current PIN">
    Type your existing PIN in the **Current PIN** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the new PIN">
    Type your new PIN in the **New PIN code** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm the new PIN">
    Re-enter the new PIN in the **Repeat new PIN code** field.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Apply the change">
    Click **Change PIN**. If the current PIN is correct and the new PIN values match, the card updates immediately and a success message appears.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Each incorrect PIN attempt counts against your remaining tries. After 5 failed attempts the card is permanently blocked.
</Warning>

## Edit label

The label is a plain-text tag stored on the card that helps you identify it. You can update it at any time.

**Label limit:** maximum 64 bytes (UTF-8 encoded).

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Edit Label">
    Click **Edit Label** in the sidebar. You will be prompted to verify your PIN first.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Enter the new label">
    Type your desired label in the **Label** field — for example, `Savings wallet`.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save">
    Click **Change it**. The application writes the label to the card and confirms success.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Check authenticity

The authenticity check verifies that your card was issued by Satochip by inspecting a certificate chain stored on the card. Three certificate levels are available:

| Level | What it represents |
|---|---|
| **Root CA certificate** | The top-level Satochip certificate authority that anchors the trust chain. |
| **Sub CA certificate** | An intermediate certificate authority signed by the Root CA. |
| **Device certificate** | The individual card's certificate, signed by the Sub CA. |

When all three certificates verify correctly, the application displays **Your card is authentic**. If verification fails, the application shows a warning and advises you to contact `support@satochip.io` if you did not load the card applet yourself.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open Check Authenticity">
    Click **Check Authenticity** in the sidebar.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Review the result">
    The authenticity status is shown immediately. Select **Root CA certificate**, **Sub CA certificate**, or **Device certificate** using the radio buttons to inspect each certificate's raw content.
  </Step>
</Steps>

## Card information

The **About** screen surfaces both software and card-level details without requiring any configuration:

- **Software information:** Satochip-Utils version and pysatochip library version.
- **Applet version:** The firmware version running on the card.
- **Label:** The card's current label (requires PIN verification).
- **Genuine:** Whether the card passed the authenticity check (requires PIN verification).
- **Card status:** Whether the card is set up, and how many PIN tries remain.
- **NFC status:** Whether NFC is enabled, disabled, or blocked.
- **Satochip configuration:** Whether the card has been seeded, and whether 2FA is enabled.

Click **Verify PIN** on the About screen to unlock the label and authenticity fields if you have not already authenticated in the current session.

## Factory reset

A factory reset erases everything on the card: private keys, the seed, the PIN, the label, and all settings. The card is returned to the same state it was in when you received it.

<Warning>
A factory reset is irreversible. All data on the card is permanently deleted. Make sure you have written down your seed phrase before proceeding — without it you cannot recover your funds.
</Warning>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Back up your seed phrase">
    Before starting, confirm you have a secure copy of your 12 or 24-word seed phrase. If you use a Seedkeeper card, verify the backup is readable.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Open Reset my Card">
    Click **Reset my Card** in the sidebar, then read the on-screen explanation. Click **Start** when ready.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Remove the card">
    A wizard dialog appears. Follow the prompt and physically remove your card from the card reader, then click the button in the dialog.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Reinsert the card">
    Insert the card again when prompted. Repeat the remove-and-reinsert cycle as many times as the wizard instructs (up to four times depending on card version).
  </Step>
  <Step title="Confirm completion">
    When the counter reaches zero, the application shows a success message and the card is fully reset. You can now set it up again from scratch.
  </Step>
</Steps>
